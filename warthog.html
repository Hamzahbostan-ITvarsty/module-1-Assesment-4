WARTHOG

Warthogs are wild members of the pig family found in grasslands and savannas across Africa. They are known for their large tusks, facial "warts," and ability to run fast with their tails sticking up. Warthogs feed on grasses, roots, and insects, and often kneel on their front legs while grazing.

Warthogs may look fierce, but they are mostly peaceful animals. They use their tusks to dig for food and to defend themselves if threatened. When danger approaches, they can run fast and often dive into burrows backward so they can face attackers with their tusks ready.

Warthogs like to roll in mud to stay cool and protect their skin from insects. They often share burrows with other animals like mongooses. Even though they belong to the pig family, warthogs are tough survivors and can live in areas where food and water are hard to find.
