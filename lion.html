LION

Lions are large carnivorous cats known as the "king of the jungle". They live in groups called prides, typically made up of related females and their offspring, along with a few males. Male lions are known for their majestic manes, while lionesses do most of the hunting. Lions are native to parts of Africa and Asia.

Lions are unique among big cats because they live in social groups. The lionesses work together to hunt animals like zebras, antelopes, and buffalo. Males defend the pride’s territory by roaring loudly and marking it with scent. A lion’s roar can be heard up to 8 kilometers away!

Cubs are born blind and are cared for by their mothers for the first few months of life. As they grow older, they learn how to hunt by watching the adults. Lions rest a lot during the day and are more active at night, making them nocturnal hunters.
