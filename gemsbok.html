GEMSBOK

The gemsbok is a large antelope species found in arid regions of Southern Africa. It has striking black-and-white facial markings and long, straight horns. Gemsboks are incredibly resilient animals, able to survive in harsh desert environments by conserving water and feeding on moisture-rich plants.

The gemsbok or gemsbuck (Oryx gazella) is a large antelope of arid regions of Southern Africa, such as the Kalahari Desert. some authorities as a subspecies.

The gemsbok is depicted on the coat of arms of Namibia, species is estimated at 373,000 individuals.

The name "gemsbok" in English is derived from Afrikaans Dutch name of the male chamois, gemsbok. Although some superficial (similarities pattern) are noticed, the chamois and the oryx are not closly related.
