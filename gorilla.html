GORILLA

Gorillas are the largest primates and are closely related to humans. They live in family groups led by a dominant male called a silverback. Gorillas are mostly gentle and shy, feeding on fruits, leaves, and stems. They use tools, exhibit emotions, and are highly intelligent animals native to the forests of Central Africa.

Gorillas are ground-dwelling, predominantly herbivorous apes that inhabit the forests of central sub-Saharan Africa. The eponymous genus Gorilla is divided into two species: the eastern gorilla and the western gorilla, and either four or five subspecies. They are the largest living primates by physical size. The DNA of gorillas is highly similar to that of humans, from 95 to 99% depending on what is counted, and they are the next closest living relatives to humans after the chimpanzees and bonobos.

Gorillas' natural habitats cover tropical or subtropical forests in Africa. Although their range covers a small percentage of Africa, gorillas cover a wide range of elevations. The mountain gorilla lives in the Albertine Rift montane cloud forests of the Virunga Volcanoes, ranging in altitude from 2,200 to 4,300 meters (7,200 to 14,100 ft). Lowland gorillas live in dense forests and lowland swamps and marshes as low as sea level. Eastern lowland gorillas live in the lowland Congo basin and eastern lowland gorilla in Central West African countries and eastern lowland gorillas in the Democratic Republic of the Congo near its border with Rwanda.
