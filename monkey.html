MONKEY

Monkeys are highly intelligent and social animals that live in groups called troops. They use vocalizations, facial expressions, and body language to communicate with one another. Monkeys are known for their agility and love of climbing, and many species live in rainforests where they leap between trees with ease.

There are over 260 species of monkeys, and they are divided into two groups: Old World monkeys (from Africa and Asia) and New World monkeys (from Central and South America). Some monkeys, like spider monkeys, use their tails to swing from branches â€” these are called prehensile tails.

Monkeys are very playful and curious. They use tools, solve problems, and even recognize themselves in mirrors. Some monkeys groom each other to build friendships and keep clean. They have a strong sense of community and often live in groups to stay safe from predators.
